# 🔐 Security Policy

### Overview

CubePay takes security seriously. We appreciate the efforts of security researchers and the community in helping us identify and address potential vulnerabilities.

### Reporting a Vulnerability

If you believe you have discovered a security vulnerability, please report it to us responsibly:

- 📧 Email: security@cubepayment.com  
- 📝 Include as much detail as possible (steps to reproduce, impact, affected components)

Please **do not disclose vulnerabilities publicly** until we have had an opportunity to investigate and resolve the issue.

### What to Expect

After submitting a report, you can expect:

- **Acknowledgement** within 3–5 business days  
- **Assessment and triage** of the reported issue  
- **Updates** on remediation progress where appropriate  

We aim to resolve valid issues as quickly as possible, depending on severity and impact.

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- Public repositories under the CubePay GitHub organization  
- SDKs, integrations, and developer tooling  

The following are **out of scope**:
- Social engineering or phishing attempts  
- Denial of service (DoS/DDoS) testing  
- Physical attacks  
- Issues requiring access to customer data or production systems  

### Responsible Disclosure Guidelines

We ask that you:

- Act in good faith and avoid privacy violations, data destruction, or service disruption  
- Only test against assets you own or are authorized to test  
- Do not exploit vulnerabilities beyond what is necessary to demonstrate the issue  
- Do not access, modify, or delete data belonging to others  

### Safe Harbor

We will not take legal action against researchers who:
- Follow this policy  
- Act in good faith  
- Report vulnerabilities responsibly  

### Disclosure Policy

- We may publicly disclose vulnerabilities after they have been resolved  
- We will coordinate disclosure timing where appropriate  

### Acknowledgements

We value contributions from the security community and may acknowledge researchers who responsibly disclose valid vulnerabilities.
